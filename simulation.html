Free energy estimation

Purpose

Calculate ΔG for each reaction and quantitatively estimate the feasibility of the RHCR. In addition, based on the calculated free energies, simulate the difference in reaction rate with and without Nicking Endonuclease in the shrinking reaction to confirm the validity of our shrinking method. The state before four-way branch migration is named P and the state after four-way branch migration is named Q. The subscript represents the number of hairpins included in main strand. In the shrinking reaction, the “polymerization actuator” is nicked (see idea). The nicked P and Q state are called P' and Q' state respectively. As depicted in figure 1, as same to the extending reaction, the initial state of shrinking reaction has two patterns, H1 end, and H2 end. And the shrinking reaction occurs by repeating those two-state.

Therefore, by calculating Δ G Q2 → P2 , Δ G Q'2 → P'2 , Δ G Q3 → P3 , Δ G Q'3 → P'3 we can generalize to Δ G Q2i → P2i , Δ G Q'2i → P'2i , Δ G Q2i+1 → P2i+1 , Δ G Q'2i+1 → P'2i+1 ( i is an integer)

fig.1 The two patterns of the shrinking reaction

i is integer. a) 2i represents even number, thus Q2i means the H2 end “polymerization actuator”. b) 2i+1 or 2i-1 represents an odd number, thus Q2i+1or Q2i-1 means the H1 end “polymerization actuator”.

Result

The free energy was calculated using the structure prediction software called NUPACK. Here, We assume that temperature is 37 degrees, 1M NaCl. However, since NUPACK returns only the most stable structure, we cannot compare the energy of the Q and P states which comprise of the same DNA species by using NPACK. Therefore, we complementary calculated the free energy in the manual using the nearest neighbor model and simultaneous equations of enthalpy and entropy( Because ΔG can be expressed as ΔG=ΔH-TΔS (ΔH: entropy. ΔS: enthalpy ) and ΔH,ΔS is temperature dependant, we can estimate the free energy at 37 degrees by solving the simultaneous equations. Only if we can obtain the desired structure in NUPACK at a certain temperature.)

The calculation of Δ G Q2 → P2

We can obtain the free energy of Q2 at 37 degrees, but the free energy of P2 could not be obtained at any temperature in NUPACK (Because of software’s properties). Thus, we calculate manually using the below model.

fig.2 The different structure part between Q2i and P2i

The domain surrounded by circle in Q state newly makes duplex in P state.

As shown in figure2, when the Q state shift to the P state, newly the purple domain forms duplex, and the red domain forms the hairpin loop. The Gibbs energy change can be obtained by subtracting the dangling end energy and adding the hairpin loop energy, duplex energy. ΔG of this reaction can be expressed by the following equation.

Δ G Q2 → P2 = Δ G Q2 - Δ G P2 = A T G C + G C C G + C C G G + HairpinLoop(n) - (CA/G) - (TG/C)

=-1.28-2.24-1.84+4.6+0.82 + 0.61 =0.67 (kcal/mol)

Here, all parameters are quoted from Santalucia et al.,(2004) which is almost the same parameters as that of NUPACK.

The calculation of Δ G Q'2 → P'2

We can obtain the free energy of Q'2 at 37 degrees, Q'2= -116.98 and the free energy of P'2 could be obtained at 38 degrees and 39 degrees. The results are -123.49(kcal/mol), -121.86(kcal/mol), respectively. We can obtain the free energy at 37 degrees by solving the simultaneous equations shown below.

{ -123.49=ΔH-311ΔS -121.86=ΔH-312ΔS

Therefore, at 37 degrees,

ΔGP'2 =ΔH-310ΔS=-116.97(kcal/mol)
Δ G Q'2 → P'2 = ΔGP'2 - ΔGQ'2 =-116.97-(-116.98)=+0.01(kcal/mol)

The calculation of Δ G Q3 → P3

We can obtain the free energy of Q3 at 37 degrees, but the free energy of P3 could not be obtained at any temperature in NUPACK (Because of software’s properties). Thus, we calculate manually using the below model.

fig.3 The different structure part between Q2i+1 and P2i+1

The domain surrounded by circle in Q state newly makes duplex in P state.

As shown in fig3, when the Q state shift to the P state, newly the light blue domain forms duplex, and the green domain forms the hairpin loop. The Gibbs energy change can be obtained by subtracting the dangling end energy and adding the hairpin loop energy, duplex energy. ΔG of this reaction can be expressed by the following equation.

Δ G Q3 → P3 = Δ G Q3 - Δ G P3 = C C G G + C T G A + T G A C + HairpinLoop(n) - (GC/G) - (GC/C)

= -1.84 -1.28 - 1.45 + 4.6 + 0.23 + 0.72 =0.98 (kcal/mol)

The calculation of Δ G Q'3 → P'3

We can obtain the free energy of P'3 at 37 degrees, P'3= -161.87 and the free energy of Q'3 could be obtained at 60 degrees and 61 degrees. The results are -112.28(kcal/mol), -110.24(kcal/mol), respectively. We can obtain the free energy at 37 degrees by solving the simultaneous equations shown below.

{ -112.28=ΔH-333ΔS -110.24=ΔH-334ΔS

Therefore, at 37 degrees,

ΔGQ'3 =ΔH-310ΔS=-159.20(kcal/mol)
Δ G Q'3 → P'3 = ΔGP'3 - ΔGQ'3 =-161.87-(-159.20)=-2.67

Summarize the obtained free energy into the below diagram. All ΔG was calculated under the condition that 37℃ and 1M NaCl.

fig.4 State Transition Diagram of the shrinking reaction (Q'2i→P'2i)( H2 end version)

fig.5 State Transition Diagram of the shrinking reaction (Q'2i+1→P'2i+1)( H1 end version)

The reaction from Q to Q' is too high to occur autonomously, but this process is driven by enzyme, which is Nicking Endonuclease. In the shrinking reaction, while the desired structure is P' , the H2 end version favors Q' than P' (fig.4). However, because ΔG is enough low to occur reversible reaction between Q' and P' , there is no problem in the shrinking reaction. We have succeeded in the theoretical explanation that DNA complex shifts to P state by nicking endonuclease.

Reaction rate simulation

We simulated RHCR extending/shrinking. The simulation model for extending is shown below.

fig.6 Schematic illustrations of the simulation model for RHCR extending

The state with only A and R is defined as Q0, then H1 hairpin binds to the toehold sequence to make P1. Next, P1 changes to Q1 through the four-way branch migration. Then, H2 hairpin binds to the Q1 toehold sequence covered with purple to make Q2 by branch migration.We regard these reactions as 1 cycle. DNA extending occurs repeating this cycle over and over again. The method of calculating the reaction rate in the simulation is described as follows. First, the reaction rate of hairpin binding is 106(mol/L)-1 in the 6 bases or more toehold sequence[DavidYu Zhang et al. (2009)]. In our design, the toehold sequence of red and green color is 10 bases, the reaction rate is considered to be 106(mol/L)-1. However, the simulation result did not match with the experiment result when we applied the value, we assumed reaction rate to be KH1=KH2=103 to fit with the experiment result.

Next, the shrinking model is shown below.

fig.7 Schematic illustrations of the simulation model for RHCR shrinking

In the above figure, Q' is the state with six bases removed by Nickase at the end of Q. Q'' is the state of Q hybridized with releasers, and Q’’’ is the state with another 6bp removed from the end bases of Q’’’. P''' is the Q’’’ after four-way branch migration, while P'' is the state of P hybridized with releasers. P' is the state of Q' after four-way branch migration. In this model, the shrinking reaction occurs by the combination of three reactions below.

  1. Nickase reaction

    The first reaction is the removal of 6 bases at the end by nickase. This reaction is irreversible reaction because of nickase activity. The precise reaction rate of nickase is expected to be determined from the experiment result, but this time because of time constrains, it was set to be 2.67×10⁻⁹L・unit⁻¹・s⁻¹ referring to the article. [Nicking enzyme-controlled toehold regulation forDNA logic circuits]

  2. Four-way branch migration

    The second reaction is the reaction from P to Q through the four-way branch migration. Since the previous section shows that the free energy change before/after the reaction is small, this reaction is considered to be reversible. If the six bases at the end is not removed by nickase, the reaction rate of the reaction is similar as the four-way branch migration of the extension reaction, corresponding to Qn+1 ↔ Pn+1 reaction in the above figure. By applying the equation of k'- k'+ = exp ( - ΔG Kb Na T ) derived from the formula of Arrhenius and the free energy change ΔG(=0.01kcal/mol) obtained in the preceding paragraph, the reaction rate k+ from P to Q and the reaction rate k-' from Q to P were set to b e 10⁻¹(sec)⁻¹ and 0.098(sec)⁻¹ respectively .

  3. Releaser-binding reaction

    The third reaction is the binding of releaser. The reaction rate is set to be 10³(sec)⁻¹ to fit with the experiment results. (The experiment was performed from 1hr till 10hr with interval 1 hr). When both the releaser-binding reaction and four-way branch migration occur, another branch migration the removal of hairpin occurs. The reaction rate kB through this reaction is set to be 10⁻¹(sec)⁻¹ because the reaction rate of the branch migration is 10⁻¹(sec)⁻¹.

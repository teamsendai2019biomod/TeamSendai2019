We will also show the figures for the supportive electrophoresis results used in the Experiment part.

Nickase working condition experiment

We performed the experiment to confirm Nickase could also work in SPSC buffer, which was used for HCR in Vencatarman et al.,2007. The result is shown below.

fig.S1 Buffer change affect Nickase work

Reaction in either 1×cutsmart buf or 1×SPSC buf for 1hr at 37 degrees. Samples were annealed before the reaction. Run in 15% polyacrylamide gel at 100v for 90mins at 4℃ with 20bp ladder. Dilution into 0.1μM. Red rectangle indicates the sample in cutsmart buf with Nickase, blue rectangle indicates the sample in SPSC buf with Nickase.

As a result, nicking did not occur in SPSC buffer although it occurred in cutsmart buffer (Lane10&12) . Furthermore, Nickase concentration does not affect nicking (lane10&13) .

RHCR extending: reaction time confirmation experiment

In order to find out how long RHCR extending takes, we performed the extra experiments. Moreover, we can deduct simulation condition with this experiment.

fig.S2 Reaction time change did not affect extending(0min~60min)

Reaction in 1×cutsmart buf for various minutes at RT. DNA hairpins were annealed before the reaction. Run in 2% agar gel at 50v for 80mins at 4℃.

fig.S3 Reaction time change affected extending(0hr~10hr)

Reaction in 1×cutsmart buf for various hours at RT. DNA hairpins were annealed before the reaction. Run in 2% agar gel at 50v for 80mins at 4℃.

fig.S4 Reaction time change affected extending(1 day~8 days)

Reaction in 1×cutsmart buf for various days at RT. DNA hairpins were annealed before the reaction. Run in 2% agar gel at 50v for 80mins at 4℃.

As a result, we concluded that RHCR extending occurs in a short time and continuously extend to 1kb. (fig.S1-S3)

RHCR reversibility confirming experiment

In order to confirm RHCR reversibility, we added extra hairpins to the after-shrinking sample. The result is shown below.

fig.S5 Reversibility examination of our RHCR system

The same sample as Fig.7 were used. Extra hairpins were added (Lane6,7, 13,17). Reaction in 1×cutsmart buf overnight at RT. Run in 2% agar gel at 50v for 80mins at 4℃.

As a result, no difference was observed between the leak reaction and re-extending (Lane6 & 17) ,suggesting HCR extending was not carried out in after-shrinking sample. There may be some problems with sequence design of our system.

Next, we performed the experiments to confirm both RHCR shrinking and reversibility. The result is shown below.

This buffer was prepared for washing BSA in cutsmart buffer. The component of buffer consist of reagent shows below. Buffer Components (pH 8.0)

fig.S6 Reversibility and shrinking examination of our RHCR system

RHCR extending was performed first. After that, both Nickase and releasers were added to the samples before the reaction. Reaction for 1hr at 37 degrees. Hairpins were annealed before the reaction. Run in 2% agar gel at 50v for 80mins at 4℃.

As a result, we could confirm that our system achieved RHCR shrinking. (Lane10 & 13), while we could not observe shrinking in lane only with hairpins.

Furthermore, we still could not confirm RHCR reversibility.

Thus, in the future, we are going to perform the experiments to observe re-extending/shrinking reaction.

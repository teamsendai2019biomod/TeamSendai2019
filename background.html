Motivation

The actuator that can extend and shrink is widely found in nature. For example, muscles whose both ends are fixed to the skeleton realize motions of body (fig. 1a). The contractile ring made of actin and myosin fixed to the cell membrane devide cells (fig. 1b). These actuators shrink or extend while maintaining the fixed points on the structure.

This kind of molecular actuator has also attracted interest in nanorobotics fields. Recently, “polymerization actuator” that can be dramatically extended by DNA hybridization chain reaction (HCR) has been demonstrated. When both ends of HCR duplex is fixed to objects, the duplex extends while keeping the connectivity between them. By applying this mechanism for a crosslink for hydrogels, 100 times swelling of the gel was realized.

However, there is a problem that the actuator can only be extended, but it cannot be shrunk if we can develop a molecular actuator that can both extend and shrink, various robotic applications become possible .

fig.1 Both ends fixed extending mechanism is widely seen in nature

Idea

We evaluated the thermo-dynamical properties of the four-way branch migration that used in the process of HCR by the Nearest-Neighbor model of DNA hybridization. Through the evaluation, we come up to the hypothesis that the four-way branch migration can be used in a reversible fashion. Based on this hypothesis, we propose a method to reverse the reaction, which realizes the shrinking.

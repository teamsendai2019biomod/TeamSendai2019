Theory

Gel electrophoresis

Electrophoresis refers to the movement of charged particles under a uniform electric field, which is dependent on the charge and size of a sample. It is a popular technique used often for DNA and RNA molecules. During electrophoresis, the particle is driven towards either cathode (+) or anode (-) due to the charged interface between the particle surface and surrounding fluid.
As for gel electrophoresis, the electric field facilitates the movement of the particle through the gel. Because how fast a molecule move is proportional to its size, smaller molecules move faster and travel further than larger ones. In addition, the mobility of the sample could be inferred based on the distance it traveled.
Although gel electrophoresis can be performed using either agarose or polyacrylamide, the polyacrylamide has a greater resolving power.

AFM

AFM (Atomic Force Microscope) is a type of scanning probe microscope(SPM) that has very-high resolution result. This microscope has resolution on the order of fractions of a nanometer, more than 1000 times better than the optical diffraction limit. Thus, it allows us to image the nano-structure like DNA.

・Pre-treatment
1. Preparation of Buffer

We used three kind of buffers for electrophoresis. All buffer except Cutsmart buffer were prepared in the lab. The components of buffer are listed below.

5X SPSC (pH 7.5)

This buffer was prepared according to Ventrakaman, 2007 paper. All the reagent below was dissolved in 1 mL of MilliQ.

Cutsmart buffer was ordered from New England Biolabs Japan in 10X buffer concentration. According to the company’s website, the component of buffer consist of reagent shows below. 1X Buffer Components (pH 7.9@25°C)

1X TAE/Mg2+ Buffer

This buffer was prepared from 10X TAE and 125 mM Mg(CH3COO)2 solution. Both solutions were mixed and diluted with MiliQ until the target concentration. We used a lot variation of Mg2+ concentration for the experiment.

Tris-EDTA buffer

This buffer was prepared for washing BSA in cutsmart buffer. The component of buffer consist of reagent shows below. Buffer Components (pH 8.0)

2.Annealing

For hairpin annealing, we have used 4 variations scheme for annealing. First, each of H1 and H2 were dissolved in 1× buffer. Then, both solutions of H1 and H2 was handled by following scheme below.

●Heat at 95oC for 2 minutes, incubate on ice for 10 minutes then leave at room temperature for 10 minutes.
●Heat at 95oC for 10 minutes, incubate on ice for 10 minutes then leave at room temperature for 10 minutes.
●Heat at 95oC for 2 minutes, incubate on ice for 2 minutes then leave at room temperature for 10 minutes.
●Heat at 95◦C for 2 minutes, slow cool at 1◦C / min to room temperature

For A·R mixture and positive reference, all the respective DNA was mixed together in 1× buffer. The mixed solution was kept at 95 °C for five minutes, and afterwards cooled down by 1 °C per minute until the temperature reached 25 °C by using Mastercycle machine.

Preparation and technique for electrophoresis of polyacrylamide gel

The preparation of 10% polyacrylamide gel and technique to do gel electrophoresis are following the standard procedures below.

1. Ammonium Persulfate (APS), 9 µL TEMED were mixed to make 9 mL/10% polyacrylamide gel.
2. Mixture was solidified for approx. 30 mins, at 25 ℃.
3. Solidified gel was set on electrophoresis chamber.
4. 1×TBE buffer running buffer was poured into electrophoresis chamber.
5. 1×ladder solution (20 bps) was prepared by mixing 1 µL 100× ladder, 19 µL 1× Buffer, and 4 µL 6×Loading buffer.
6. 0.6 µL 6×Loading buffer was added for each 3 µL of mixture DNA.
7. 3.6 µL of each final mixture was applied into each well.
8. 100 V voltage was applied for an hour in room temperature.
9. Gel was stained by SYBR GOLD for 20 minutes.
10. Fluorescence was observed by gel imager (ChemiDoc MP, BIO-RAD, USA).

●Preparation and technique for electrophoresis of agarose gel

The preparation of 2% agarose gel and technique to do gel electrophoresis are following the standard procedures below.

1. 0.5 g of Agarose S was dissolved into 25 mL of 0.5xTBE/5 mM Mg2+ to make 25 mL/2% agarose gel.
2. Mixture was solidified for approx. 60 mins, at 25 ℃.
3. Solidified gel was set on electrophoresis chamber.
4. 0.5×TBE buffer running buffer was poured into electrophoresis chamber.
5. 1×ladder solution (100 bps) was prepared by mixing 1 µL 100× ladder, 19 µL 1× Buffer, and 4 µL 6×Loading buffer.
6. Final mixture was prepared by mixing 4 µL DNA solution, 1 µL 50% glycerol and 1 µL 6×Loading buffer.
7. 6 µL of each final mixture DNA was applied into each well.
8. 50 V voltage was applied for 80 minutes in fridge (4oC).
9. Gel was stained by SYBR GOLD for 20 minutes.
10. Fluorescence was observed by gel imager (ChemiDoc MP, BIO-RAD, USA).
11. Gel was washed in MiliQ for 10 minutes.
12. Gel was observed again by gel imager.

1. The image was analyzed using software Fiji.

4.To analyze each lane, the lane was selected using the rectangle function
5. The fluorescence data from each lane was obtained using both plot line function and integrated density function.

AFM Imaging

For observing the nano-scale structures of our RHCR products, we used Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM). The process of observation is listed below.

1. Samples were prepared for observation. Dilution into around 10nM with Tris-EDTA buffer.
2. Diluted sample was put on the base made of mica, and leave it for minutes at RT.
3. APTES(0.05%) was added to the sample.
4. After minutes, the sample was washed with Tris-EDTA buffer.
5. Samples were observed with AFM (OLYMPUS DP80).
